# Security Policy

## Supported Versions

| Version | Supported |
|---------|-----------|
| Latest  | ✅        |

---

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you've found a security vulnerability in SharkEyes — please **do not open a public issue**.

Report it privately via email:

**hello@sharkeyes.dev**

We take all reports seriously. Here's what to expect:

- **48 hours** — confirmation that we received your report
- **7 days** — initial assessment and severity evaluation
- We'll keep you updated on the fix progress
- We will **not** take legal action against researchers acting in good faith

---

## What to Include in Your Report

- Description of the vulnerability and its potential impact
- Steps to reproduce (proof of concept)
- Affected component (API, JS widget, dashboard, etc.)
- Your contact info for follow-up

---

## Scope

**In scope:**
- `sharkeyes.dev`
- SharkEyes API endpoints
- JavaScript verification widget

**Out of scope:**
- Websites of customers using SharkEyes
- Third-party infrastructure
- DoS/DDoS attacks
- Social engineering

---

## Ground Rules

To qualify as good-faith research, please:

- Do not access or modify other users' data
- Do not disrupt service availability
- Do not perform automated scanning without prior agreement
- Do not disclose the vulnerability publicly before we've confirmed the fix

---

## Our Commitment

We believe security researchers make the internet safer. If you follow these rules, we commit to:

- Working with you to understand and fix the issue
- Crediting you (if you'd like) after the fix is released
- Not pursuing legal action for good-faith research
